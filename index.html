Research context

The problem

Dominant deepfake detection methods rely on extrinsic signals — provenance metadata, cryptographic hashes, model fingerprints. These are brittle: a re-encoded or unwatermarked video evades them entirely. They depend on the closed-world assumption that authentic content is pre-certified at source.

The argument

Content-level signals exploit generative pipeline failure modes — constraints that models cannot easily satisfy because they require simulating physics, biology, or cross-modal coherence. These signals are intrinsic to the video content itself, making them more durable as generative models advance.

Robustness insight

Spatial artifacts are being rapidly eliminated by diffusion-based models. Physiological signals — rPPG, pupillary response, respiration — require simulating biology and remain the most robust category. The field's detection capacity is eroding fastest exactly where it is most invested.

Scope

This taxonomy is scoped to video deepfakes and content-level signals only, excluding audio-only deepfakes, text-based manipulation, and extrinsic detection methods. Real-time synthesis in video calls is identified as the key frontier for 2026.
